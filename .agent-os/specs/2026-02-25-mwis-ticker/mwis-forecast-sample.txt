Headline for Lake District

Increasingly wet and foggy; blustery

How windy? (On the summits)

Southwesterly 30-40mph, notable gusts including to lower slopes. A tendency towards sustained speeds near the higher end of the range with time.

Effect of the wind on you?

Strenuous-to-arduous walking with considerable wind chill in exposure. Buffeting gusts often challenge stability, including to some middle slopes.

How Wet?

Increasingly rainy

From dawn, patchy drizzle affects the high fells, mostly south and west Lakeland. With time, patchy rain arrives and becomes more frequent with patches extending more broadly.

Cloud on the hills?

Fairly extensive

Fog shrouds the fells from middle slopes upward, often to the lower slopes of the Scafell group and towards Coniston. Best chance of higher breaks near Catbells/Blencathra, but unlikely to summits, and more extensively shrouded here with time as well.

20%

Sunshine and air clarity?

A few brighter periods towards north/east Lakeland in the morning, otherwise overcast and dull. Good visibility where out of cloud, but otherwise poor.

How Cold? (at 750m)

6C, very little change with added height, if any. Lowering slightly overnight. Feeling like -4 to -6C in strongest direct wind.

Freezing Level
